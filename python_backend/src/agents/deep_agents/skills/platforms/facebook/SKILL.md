# Enterprise Brand Facebook - 2025

## Platform Role for Brands

Facebook for enterprise brands focuses on:
- Brand community building
- Product announcements and launches
- Customer engagement and service
- Event and campaign coverage
- Paid and organic integration
- Local market content

## Content Types

### Product Announcements
- New product launches
- Collection previews
- Limited edition releases
- Restock notifications

### Brand Content
- Campaign assets
- Brand storytelling
- Behind-the-scenes
- Sustainability and values

### Community Content
- Customer spotlights
- User-generated content (curated)
- Community questions
- Milestone celebrations

### Event Coverage
- Live event streams
- Event recaps
- Store openings
- Fashion shows and presentations

### Service Content
- Store updates
- Hours and availability
- Service announcements
- FAQ content

## Post Structure

### Product Announcement
```
[Product name or headline]

[Key benefit and features]

[Availability and pricing]

[CTA with link]

#BrandName #ProductName
```

### Brand Story Post
```
[Hook - compelling opening]

[Story - brand narrative or behind-the-scenes]

[Connection - why it matters to audience]

[Soft CTA]

#BrandName
```

### Community Post
```
[Question or prompt]

[Context or relevance]

[Invitation to share]

#BrandName
```

## Visual Standards

### Image Content
- High-quality product photography
- Lifestyle imagery
- Campaign visuals
- Professional event coverage

### Video Content
- Product demonstrations
- Behind-the-scenes
- Campaign videos
- Live streams (events, launches)

### Video Best Practices
- 15-60 seconds for feed videos
- Native upload (not YouTube links)
- Captions for muted viewing
- Strong first 3 seconds

## Facebook Reels for Brands

### Appropriate Content
- Product showcases
- Quick tutorials
- Behind-the-scenes moments
- Collection teasers

### Production Quality
- Professional footage
- Clean editing
- Brand-consistent aesthetic
- Subtle text overlays

### Avoid
- Viral trends that don't fit
- Low-quality content
- Excessive effects

## Hashtag Strategy

### Brand Hashtags
- #BrandName (always)
- #CampaignName (when active)
- #ProductLine (when relevant)

### Guidelines
- 1-3 hashtags maximum
- End of post placement
- Avoid trending/viral tags

## Community Management

### Response Guidelines
- Professional, helpful tone
- Timely responses
- Move complaints to private
- Thank positive feedback

### Engagement Approach
- Respond to comments
- Acknowledge shares
- Answer product questions
- Address concerns professionally

### What to Share
- High-quality UGC (with permission)
- Positive customer stories
- Community milestones

## Facebook Groups (Brand Communities)

### Group Purpose
- Product discussions
- Community building
- Early access/VIP content
- Customer feedback

### Content Mix
- Product updates and previews
- Exclusive offers
- Community polls
- Member spotlights

### Moderation
- Clear community guidelines
- Professional moderation
- Timely removal of spam
- Constructive conversation fostering

## Live Video for Brands

### Appropriate Uses
- Product launches
- Event coverage
- Q&A with experts
- Behind-the-scenes tours

### Production Standards
- Professional setup
- Good lighting and audio
- Brand-appropriate backdrop
- Prepared content/talking points

## Paid-Organic Integration

### Organic Content That Works for Paid
- Product launches
- Collection showcases
- Campaign hero content
- Strong visual content

### Boosting Strategy
- Boost high-performing organic
- Support campaign launches
- Drive traffic for key initiatives
- Retarget engaged audiences

## Posting Strategy

### Frequency
- 1-2 posts per day maximum
- Quality over quantity
- More during campaigns

### Timing
- Business hours for announcements
- Evening for lifestyle content
- Real-time for events

### Content Mix
- 50% Product/Promotional
- 30% Brand/Lifestyle
- 20% Community/Engagement
