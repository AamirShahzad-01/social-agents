# Enterprise Brand Twitter/X - 2025

## Platform Role for Brands

Twitter/X for enterprise brands focuses on:
- Brand announcements and news
- Product launches and availability
- Real-time marketing (events, culture)
- Customer engagement and service
- Campaign amplification

## Content Types for Brands

### Announcements
- Product launches
- Collection drops
- Partnership reveals
- Corporate news

### Product Content
- New arrivals
- Restocks
- Limited editions
- Seasonal highlights

### Campaign Amplification
- Campaign launch tweets
- Behind-the-scenes teasers
- Cross-platform promotion
- Hashtag campaigns

### Real-Time Marketing
- Cultural moments (aligned with brand)
- Live events coverage
- Awards and achievements
- Industry news reactions

### Customer Engagement
- Community responses
- User-generated content shares
- Customer appreciation
- Service updates

## Tweet Structure for Brands

### Announcement Tweet
```
[Clear headline statement]

[Supporting detail or context]

[Availability/CTA]

[Link if applicable]

#BrandName #Product
```

### Product Tweet
```
[Product name or collection]
[Key feature or benefit]
[Where to shop]

#BrandName
```

### Campaign Tweet
```
[Campaign message or tagline]

[Visual description or link]

#CampaignHashtag #BrandName
```

When more detail is needed:

```

Tweet 2: Key details or story context

Tweet 3-5: Feature highlights or story beats

Final Tweet: CTA and availability
```

## Visual Standards

### Image Content
- High-quality product photography
- Campaign imagery
- Clean, professional visuals
- On-brand color palette

### Video Content
- Short product clips (under 60 sec)
- Campaign trailers
- Behind-the-scenes glimpses
- Event coverage

### GIFs
- Use sparingly
- Brand-appropriate only
- Professional quality

## Hashtag Strategy

### Brand Hashtags
- #BrandName (always)
- #CampaignName (when active)
- #ProductLine (when relevant)

### Usage Guidelines
- 1-2 hashtags per tweet
- Hashtags at end of tweet
- No hashtag stuffing
- Avoid viral trending hashtags

## Tone & Voice

### Characteristics
- Confident and direct
- Professional but personable
- Responsive and engaged
- Culturally aware (when appropriate)

### Language
- Clear, concise statements
- Active voice
- Benefit-focused product descriptions
- Consistent brand terminology

### Avoid
- Trying too hard to be "relatable"
- Forced humor
- Viral meme formats
- Engagement bait

## Real-Time Marketing Guidelines

### When to Engage
- Major cultural events aligned with brand
- Industry awards and recognition
- Sports events (if brand-relevant)
- Positive brand mentions from celebrities/influencers

### How to Engage
- Authentic brand voice
- Value-add commentary
- Timely but quality-first
- When in doubt, don't tweet

### When NOT to Engage
- Tragedies or sensitive news
- Political controversies
- Trends that don't fit brand
- Just to be part of conversation

## Customer Engagement

### Response Guidelines
- Professional, helpful tone
- Move service issues to DM
- Acknowledge positive feedback
- Timely responses (within hours)

### What to Share
- High-quality UGC (with permission)
- Celebrity/influencer organic mentions
- Customer testimonials

## Posting Strategy

### Frequency
- 2-5 tweets per day
- More during campaigns/launches
- Less is more for quality

### Timing
- Business hours for announcements
- Peak engagement times for campaigns
- Real-time for events
